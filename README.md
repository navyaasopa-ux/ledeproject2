### Project 2:
This project displays COVID-19 misinformation patterns across countries, sources, and narratives, conducted as part of the Lede Data Program at Columbia University in 2026.

## Overview

The project looks at how misinformation spread through the pandemic era, who was behind it, where it appeared and circulated most frequently, and what patterns emerged from the distinct types of misinformation.

I combined analysis of the clean dataset with visual analysis of the story. The goal was to make a cohesive argument on the mechanisms and drivers of misinformation during a global crisis.

### Live Project:

## Core Questions

* Where did misinformation originate from?
* What kind of misinformation was it?
* Who was actually spreading misinformation- individuals, public figures, or companies?
* Were certain platforms more prone to it than others?
* What was the false narrative that was being set?

---

## The Data

The dataset includes over 5,600 instances of misinformation categorized by:

* Source type: Individuals, Influencers, Companies, etc.
* Platform: WhatsApp, Twitter, Facebook, YouTube, etc.
* Countries: India, USA, China, etc.
* Motive: Politics, Fear, Profit, etc.
* Misinformation Type: False Reporting, Conspiracy, Fake Remedy, etc.
